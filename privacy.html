Privacy

Most privacy policies explain what a company does with your data. This one explains why there is no company holding any.

Last updated 23 July 2026 · Applies to Curio on the web, iOS and Android

The short version

The Curio is a diary that runs entirely on your own device. It has no user accounts, no server, no database in the cloud, no analytics and no advertising. Your entries, photos, recordings and settings are written to your device's own storage and stay there.

The honest test

Curio contains no code capable of uploading your diary. You do not have to take that on trust — the source is on the page you are reading this from, and you can watch the network traffic yourself in any browser's developer tools.

The two places anything leaves your device

Being honest about the exceptions matters more than the general claim, so here they are in full. Both are optional, both are off unless you turn them on, and neither carries anything about you.

1 · Live dictation

If you switch on live transcription, your browser's own speech service converts speech to text. On most browsers — Chrome especially — this sends audio to the browser maker's servers, not to us. Curio tells you this before you enable it and it is off by default. Audio recording is always local; only dictation involves anyone else.

2 · Today in History enrichment

If you switch this on, Curio asks Wikipedia's public feed for events on a given date. The request contains a month and a day and nothing else — no identifier, no diary content, nothing about you. It is off by default and the bundled almanac works without it.

That is the complete list. Everything else — the writing, the patterns, the search, the holidays, the meeting write-ups, the annual book — happens on your device with the radio off.

Cookies and tracking

Curio sets no cookies. It uses no analytics, no advertising networks, no tracking pixels, no fingerprinting and no third-party scripts of any kind. There is no consent banner because there is nothing to consent to.

Your rights, and how to exercise them

Data protection law gives you rights over personal data an organisation holds about you. Curio holds none — but the equivalent controls are built into the app and take one tap each.

Because nothing is transmitted to us, there is no request to make and no waiting period. Uninstalling the app removes the data with it, so export a Recovery Kit before you do.

Regulatory position

GDPR and UK GDPR

Curio processes personal data solely on your own device and under your sole control. No personal data is transmitted to, stored by, or accessible to the developer. There are no international transfers because there are no transfers. Where a purely personal or household activity is concerned, Article 2(2)(c) applies.

CCPA / CPRA

No personal information is collected, sold or shared. There is nothing to opt out of.

Children

Curio is not directed at children under 13 and collects no information from anyone, of any age. No account can be created because there are no accounts.

Security

Recovery Kits are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a key derived from your passphrase by PBKDF2-SHA256 at 310,000 iterations. Your passphrase never leaves your device and cannot be recovered by anyone, including us. An optional PIN locks the app on opening.

Because your archive lives on one device, its greatest risk is not theft but loss. Keep a Recovery Kit somewhere you trust.
