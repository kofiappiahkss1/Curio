What it does

Photos catch the highlights. Everything else evaporates.

The meal you keep reordering, the walk you didn't think to remember, the article that changed your mind — gone by the weekend. Curio keeps the texture, not just the peaks.

Snap it in the app

A real camera inside Curio — takes the picture, shrinks it, and files it with a placard. Or pick one you already have.

Places, meals, people

Name a place the way you'd say it to a friend. Curio remembers how often you return, and tells you.

Four sealed subjects

Banking, health, messages, passwords. If something you type looks like one, it is refused before it is written — and shown as withheld.

No server to breach

There is no account and no backend. Curio contains no code that could upload anything — a stronger promise than a privacy policy.

Yours to take

Export as one plain file, or as readable text you could print. Nothing is locked in, ever.

Survives a lost phone

If the worst happens

Lose the phone. Keep the diary.

Curio has no server — which is the whole point, and also the obvious risk. So it gives you something better than a server: a key that only you hold.

1

Choose a passphrase

Four or five unrelated words. Curio shows a short fingerprint so you can check later that you wrote down the right one. We never see it and cannot reset it.

2

Keep a Recovery Kit

One encrypted file holding everything — placards, photos, moods, language. Put it in Drive, iCloud, or email it to yourself. It's unreadable without your passphrase.

3

Open Curio anywhere

New phone, borrowed laptop, any browser. Choose Restore, pick the kit, type the passphrase. Your whole diary is back — and merges rather than overwrites.

It can back itself up, too

On Android and desktop, point Curio at a folder your cloud already syncs. It quietly rewrites the encrypted kit as you go — nothing to remember, nothing to press. On iPhone, Curio reminds you before the gap gets dangerous.

What Curio will not pretend to do

It cannot silently watch your screen. No app can, on any phone — iOS forbids it outright, and Android demands a visible notification plus fresh consent every session. Instead, share or paste anything into Curio and it becomes a memory.

Capture is a deliberate tap, not surveillance. Given what this app holds, that constraint is arguably the right one.

Your archive lives and dies with the phone. That is the price of having no server. There is an Export button — use it now and then.
